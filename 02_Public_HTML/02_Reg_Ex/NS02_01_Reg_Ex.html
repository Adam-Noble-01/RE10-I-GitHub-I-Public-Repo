Overview

Escaping Special Characters

In regex, certain characters (like the dot or plus sign) are treated as special symbols. To match these characters literally, we need to escape them with a backslash ().

Grouping and Capturing

Use parentheses () to group parts of the regex. Grouping helps to apply quantifiers to larger parts of the pattern or capture specific portions of the match for later use (e.g., in replacements).

List Matching: Detailed Example

Use the following expression to target sublist items specifically, ignoring top-level items:

Regex: ^\s*\d+.\d+.

This matches items like 1.1., 1.2., 2.3. but leaves top-level items like 1. or 2. unchanged.

Customising for a Specific List Level

To match sublist items under a specific top-level item (e.g., only 1.x. sublist items), adjust the pattern:

Regex: ^\s*1.\d+.

This will target sublist items like 1.1., 1.2., while ignoring sublists under other numbers such as 2.1..
